# Attribution

The notes under `reference/empirical-grounding/` were adapted from a publicly available knowledge-management research compilation distributed under the MIT license. They compress decades of personal-knowledge-management literature into actionable claims about cognitive constraints, scaling regimes, failure modes, atom design, wikilink semantics, MOC construction, processing workflows, and domain-specific reference templates.

This plugin curates ~60 of those notes as on-demand reference material — not as plugin source code. They are consulted by skills (especially `/audit` and `/anneal`) when a specific empirical principle is relevant to a decision the model is making. They are not loaded into context every session.

Where empirical-grounding notes carry their own internal references and provenance fields, those are preserved as authentic provenance of the original research material. The plugin's own design and implementation (verbs, data model, presets, tools, the design principles in `DESIGN.md`) are independent.

If you adapt this plugin further, please carry this attribution forward.
